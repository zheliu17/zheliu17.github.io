Theoretical Chemistry
Mixed Quantum-Classical Liouville Dynamics in Position Representation
This project and publications are in progress. Title and contents may change.
Advisor, Prof. Gabriel Hanna Nov. 2019 - Present
The quantum-classical Liouville equation (QCLE) allows us to calculate the quantum dynamics of the subsystem exactly, which is bilinearly couple with the classic environments, e.g., the surface hopping problem. Our newly developed algorithms have been proved that can solve the QCLE with high accuracy and low computational cost. Now we are interested in some theoretical and practical models (e.g., the Azzouz-Borgis picture) and trying to apply our new methodology into these problems.

Theoretical Studies on the Structures and Properties of TiO2 Surface Adsorbed by Metal Atom
This project is my undergraduate graduation thesis. Language of thesis is not in English. Contact me for full thesis. Slides are available here.
Advisor, Prof. Hongjun Fan Jan. 2019 - June 2019
By absorbing transition metals on the surface, the photocatalytic properties of titanium dioxide (Rutile) may increase. In this research, we calculated the single atom loaded rutile surface. We studied three different surfaces, stoichiometric TiO2 and two different reduced TiO2 (In plane oxygen vacancy and bridging oxygen vacancy. The studies of the first one is insufficient). The results provided us the adsorption energy of metals, geometry and electronic structures, and potential catalytic properties of the loaded surfaces. We also discussed the difference between different metals and absorption sites.
Probing reactions of the hydroxyl radical in aqueous environments
Collaborator: Carlos Enriquez-Victorero
Advisor, Prof. Peter Kusalik July 2018 - Oct. 2018
We performed ab-initio molecular dynamics simulations (Car–Parrinello molecular dynamics, CPMD) for hydroxyl radical and chlorine in aqueous solution. The CPMD method introduced the fictitious electron mass to prevent time-consuming self-consistent calculation in each time step. The simulations provided a description of the geometry and electronics structures of hydroxyl radical and chlorine, and the interactions between them and water molecules. Our results helped us understand the influence of different electron mass.
Theoretical studies on the solvated structures and properties of lithium ions in dimethyl carbonate
Advisor, Prof. Hongjun Fan June 2017 - June 2018
We have noted an abnormal increasing of the coordination number of lithium in Dimethyl Carbonate (DMC, a commonly used electrolyte in Lithium batteries). To investigate this phenomenon, we calculated the solvation energies and structures of the lithium solution complex. We also proposed some improvement methods for our previous coordination number measurement.
